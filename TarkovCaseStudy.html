ESCAPE FROM TARKOV

Survive the Interface: Redesigning Tarkov’s Inventory UX for Speed and Clarity

A partial reimagining of Escape from Tarkov’s interface to reduce friction, enhance user focus, and maintain immersion in one of the most intense looter-shooters on the market.

Problem & Goals

Escape from Tarkov’s interface introduces unnecessary friction in both survival and social systems. Players must open the inventory for basic real-time information, like health, hydration, buffs, or grenade type, interrupting the flow of gameplay. Social features are also limited or inefficient — there’s no way to view online friends, create squads, or message teammates easily. Combined with missing presets and redundant workflows, even simple tasks like buying or crafting items become slow and frustrating.

  • Reduce time spent on essential actions like buying, crafting, and communicating
  • Surface key survival stats without breaking immersion
  • Improve the visibility and flow of social features (online status, team invites, messaging)
  • Streamline the interface to support faster decisions in high-stakes scenarios

Research & Discovery

To identify key UI and accessibility pain points in Tarkov by gathering feedback from diverse players, focusing on improving information clarity, social features, and inventory interactions.

Key findings from User Interviews

  • Information Gaps: Players struggle to find critical status info (buffs/debuffs, hydration, health, compass) without opening the inventory.
  • Social Friction: Friend list lacks online status visibility and makes group creation unintuitive; players can’t send invites from raid lobbies.
  • Grenade Frustration: 80% of players throw grenades without knowing the type; only a few bind grenade types manually at raid start.
  • Marketplace Overload: Majority spend their time in the Flea Market selling, which is repetitive and poorly optimized.
  • Crafting Inefficiency: Users must manually look up crafting recipes each time, causing unnecessary friction.
  • Quest Confusion: Players frequently search third-party websites for quest locations, indicating a lack of in-game guidance.

Key Insights

  • Tarkov’s full HUD hide feature offers unmatched immersion control compared to competitors.
  • Dark and Darker improves player awareness with buffs/debuffs and compass info in the HUD, reducing frustration.
  • Warzone provides robust social features with easy friend status visibility and group management, lacking in Tarkov.
  • Players want quicker access to grenade/healing selection and always-visible critical stats without opening the inventory.
  • UI improvements like money value tooltips and message separation enhance clarity during gameplay.

UX User Flow

The flow begins with launching the game and preparing the loadout before entering a raid. During combat, the player may get shot, prompting a health check and decision whether healing is needed. If yes, the healing wheel is accessed via shortcut for quick application. Grenade use is similarly streamlined through a grenade wheel allowing fast selection and deployment. These interaction points reduce downtime, increase responsiveness, and improve player focus in intense moments.

Design Simplification & Early Concepts

Friend System Overhaul

Improved friend management and clarity by adding status icons, direct friend requests, and group functionality. Designed to reduce friction and support social interaction

Pre-Raid Group Coordination

Streamlined team readiness with a "Ready" button for all members. Added friend invite/kick options and real-time friend list access to improve flow before raids.

Raid UI Enhancements

Boosted in-raid awareness with always-visible health, buffs/debuffs, and optional overlays (weather, compass, time). Introduced a contextual grenade wheel with focus blur for clarity.

PostFX & Flea Market QoL

Reduced screen switching by embedding item filtering and trading actions directly into context. Users can now manage PostFX presets intuitively.

Settings Enhancements

Identified friction in visual and market settings. Introduced PostFX presets for faster customization and a streamlined flea market overlay to reduce navigation steps and speed up item interactions.

High-Fidelity Design

Friend System Overhaul

Goal: Reduce social friction and improve clarity.

Key Changes:

  • Clear status indicators (online, in raid, away, hidden)
  • Direct friend request and accept flows
  • Group creation with public/private toggle

Pre-Raid Group Coordination

Goal: Improve team flow and readiness.

Key Changes:

  • "Ready" state required for all members before raid
  • Kick/invite controls embedded in group panel
  • Inline access to friend list during prep

Raid UI Enhancements

Goal: Improve player awareness and combat efficiency.

Key Changes:

  • Always-visible health bar with debuffs
  • Overlay UI for grenade/heal wheels (with focus blur)
  • Top HUD strip for time, compass, and weather

Settings Enhancements

Goal: Reduce repetitive visual setup and streamline trading.

Key Changes:

  • PostFX presets: create, save, delete configurations
  • Contextual flea market: item filter opens inline
  • "Place Offer" opens instantly without screen switch

Flea Market

Goal: Minimize screen-switching and improve item interactions.

Key Changes:

  • Embedded item filter results without leaving stash
  • Auto-list offers and quick "Place Offer" action from the same screen
  • Presets allow users to instantly apply favorite graphic settings

Retrospective and Learnings

This project started as a visual polish effort but quickly evolved into a broader UX rethink. By focusing on real user friction points — from tedious item management to frustrating raid interactions — I prioritized meaningful changes that enhanced both usability and immersion. The process highlighted the importance of user feedback, smart scope management, and iterating with purpose.

Key Takeaways

What began as a plan to fix a few tedious UI elements quickly expanded as I noticed deeper friction in small interactions throughout the game. Once I implemented the first changes and users experienced real relief in their workflows, feedback began pouring in with suggestions for other improvements. This shift taught me to listen actively and reevaluate priorities based on user pain.

One of the biggest challenges was scoping the case study — deciding what to include and what to leave for future iterations. I had to balance ambition with clarity, focusing on features that impacted the core user loop the most: friend list usability, raid interaction clarity, healing/grenade access, and smoother market & crafting flows.

What I Improved

  • Simplified the Friend List with clearer interactions, statuses, and fewer pop-ups
  • Improved Pre-Raid Coordination with real-time friend status and faster team setup
  • Gave users more contextual info during raids, while keeping it optional for experienced players
  • Reduced time sink in the Flea Market and PostFX areas by avoiding unnecessary screen switches
  • Introduced presets and overlays for faster, less tedious customization

Lessons Learned

  • User feedback is invaluable — even small changes can spark major input.
  • UI clarity reduces frustration dramatically in high-stakes environments like Tarkov.
  • Simplifying access to frequent actions (like grenades or healing) saves lives in-game — and reduces player churn.
  • Microinteractions and removing redundancy have outsized impact in complex systems.
  • Transparency and iteration are key to building trust with a dedicated user base.

If I Had More Time

With more time and space, I'd:

  • Improve the Stash UX with better iconography and personalization
  • Rework inventory pop-ups (like bags/cases) into smarter collapsible modules
  • Add more user profile customization
  • Continue gathering player feedback focused on discoverability and future features

Escape from Tarkov has a passionate community and a UX foundation with great potential.
