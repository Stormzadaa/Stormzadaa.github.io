ESCAPE FROM TARKOV

Survive the Interface: Redesigning Tarkov’s Inventory UX for Speed and Clarity

A partial reimagining of Escape from Tarkov’s interface to reduce friction, enhance user focus, and maintain immersion in one of the most intense looter-shooters on the market.

Problem & Goals

Escape from Tarkov’s interface introduces unnecessary friction in both survival and social systems. Players must open the inventory for basic real-time information, like health, hydration, buffs, or grenade type, interrupting the flow of gameplay. Social features are also limited or inefficient — there’s no way to view online friends, create squads, or message teammates easily. Combined with missing presets and redundant workflows, even simple tasks like buying or crafting items become slow and frustrating.

  • Reduce time spent on essential actions like buying, crafting, and communicating
  • Surface key survival stats without breaking immersion
  • Improve the visibility and flow of social features (online status, team invites, messaging)
  • Streamline the interface to support faster decisions in high-stakes scenarios

Research & Discovery

To identify key UI and accessibility pain points in Tarkov by gathering feedback from diverse players, focusing on improving information clarity, social features, and inventory interactions.

Key findings from User Interviews

  • Information Gaps: Players struggle to find critical status info (buffs/debuffs, hydration, health, compass) without opening the inventory.
  • Social Friction: Friend list lacks online status visibility and makes group creation unintuitive; players can’t send invites from raid lobbies.
  • Grenade Frustration: 80% of players throw grenades without knowing the type; only a few bind grenade types manually at raid start.
  • Marketplace Overload: Majority spend their time in the Flea Market selling, which is repetitive and poorly optimized.
  • Crafting Inefficiency: Users must manually look up crafting recipes each time, causing unnecessary friction.
  • Quest Confusion: Players frequently search third-party websites for quest locations, indicating a lack of in-game guidance.

To benchmark Tarkov’s UI, I analyzed similar games across key areas like crafting, in-raid feedback, and social systems. This revealed clear usability gaps and informed design opportunities.

Key Insights

  • Tarkov’s full HUD hide feature offers unmatched immersion control compared to competitors.
  • Dark and Darker improves player awareness with buffs/debuffs and compass info in the HUD, reducing frustration.
  • Warzone provides robust social features with easy friend status visibility and group management, lacking in Tarkov.
  • Players want quicker access to grenade/healing selection and always-visible critical stats without opening the inventory.
  • UI improvements like money value tooltips and message separation enhance clarity during gameplay.

Ideate and Design

The flow begins with launching the game and preparing the loadout before entering a raid. During combat, the player may get shot, prompting a health check and decision whether healing is needed. If yes, the healing wheel is accessed via shortcut for quick application. Grenade use is similarly streamlined through a grenade wheel allowing fast selection and deployment. These interaction points reduce downtime, increase responsiveness, and improve player focus in intense moments.
